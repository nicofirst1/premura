# Runtime orchestrator and operating roles

Premura's runtime multi-agent surface will distinguish the **orchestrator** from **operating roles**: the orchestrator routes a human's health-data goal, sequences role handoffs, and records a compact trace, while declaration-driven roles such as `ingest`, `analysis`, `human_facing`, `answer_audit`, and `improvement_scan` own bounded responsibilities and governance surfaces. Cross-role handoffs go through the orchestrator, health-interpretive answers are audited before final narration, and product gaps found during operation become private improvement candidates before any user-approved issue or dev-time workflow. This keeps runtime operation separate from bootstrap/setup and from codebase extension, preserves traceability without hidden role memory, and avoids turning runtime roles into persona names or an unbounded router switch.
